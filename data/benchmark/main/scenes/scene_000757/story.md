# scene_000757

The Tropical Cyclone Intensity reached extreme values, measured at over 120 knots, as the storm system intensified over warm ocean waters. When these sustained winds reached such catastrophic velocities, they drove the Extremely Low Central Pressure Indicator to activate-signaling that the storm's core had fallen below critical pressure thresholds, often accompanying Category 4 and 5 hurricanes. This combination of extreme rotational force and dramatically reduced core pressure ultimately triggered a Severe Wind Gusts Event, with brief wind speed spikes surging well beyond the sustained measurements, causing devastation through microbursts and eyewall turbulence. Even storms with moderate Tropical Cyclone Intensity occasionally showed elevated central pressure readings that did not meet the extreme threshold, though Severe Wind Gusts Events remained most frequent when both the intensity and pressure indicator aligned.
