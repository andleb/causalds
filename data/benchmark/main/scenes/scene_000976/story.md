# scene_000976

During the summer monsoon season, meteorologists monitored the Atmospheric Electrical Instability Index as it climbed to critical levels above 8.5, indicating severe convective conditions ripe for thunderstorm development. This atmospheric turbulence drove the Cloud-to-Ground Electric Potential upward to 120 kV, creating an enormous voltage differential between the storm clouds and the ancient temple's spire. The combination of extreme atmospheric instability and high electric potential generated a devastating Lightning Strike Current of 185 kA that struck the temple's wooden roof structure. The intense thermal energy from this massive current transfer immediately caused Roof Timber Ignition throughout the cedar shakes and structural beams. Ultimately, the total Temple Fire Damage Area reached 340 square meters, a severity that depended both on the raw power of the initial lightning discharge and the subsequent spread of flame from the ignited roof timbers into the interior prayer halls.
