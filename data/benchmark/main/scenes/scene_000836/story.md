# scene_000836

In the shale basin development project, the initiation of intensive **Hydraulic Fracturing Operations** marked the beginning of multiple environmental risk pathways. The high-pressure injection of fracturing fluids at depths exceeding two kilometers generated substantial mechanical stresses on well infrastructure, leading to **Well Integrity Failure** characterized by compromised cement bonds and casing breaches. These **Hydraulic Fracturing Operations** also introduced direct contamination risks through surface spills of flowback fluids and the creation of artificial fracture networks that potentially intersected with shallow drinking water aquifers. When **Well Integrity Failure** occurred, the damaged well barriers created conduits for methane and residual fracturing chemicals to migrate upward from production zones, independently contributing to resource degradation. Consequently, monitoring stations detected **Groundwater Contamination** in domestic wells proximal to the development site, with the contamination stemming from both the operational activities themselves and the mechanical failures they precipitated.
