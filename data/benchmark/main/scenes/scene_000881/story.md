# scene_000881

The expeditionary force identified an insurgent strongpoint exhibiting a Defensive Position Fortification Level of 7.8 on the engineering assessment scale, indicating subterranean reinforced concrete emplacements hardened against indirect fire. Concurrently, an Administrative Processing Delay of 6.2 hours developed as the fires request packet awaited judge advocate general review and intelligence validation at corps headquarters, degrading the temporal advantage of the targeting opportunity and forcing abbreviated mission planning cycles.

When determining whether the Mortar Attack Authorized status should be granted, the tactical operations center weighed the elevated Defensive Position Fortification Level-which demanded heavy 120mm mortar munitions to achieve meaningful penetration-against the operational risks introduced by the Administrative Processing Delay, which had compressed the decision window to minutes before the enemy's predicted displacement. The Mortar Attack Authorized designation was ultimately approved, though the lingering Administrative Processing Delay had eliminated the possibility of pre-registered fire missions and forced reliance on observed fire adjustments.

The engagement produced Structural Damage Severity measuring 34 percentage damaged to the primary bunker complex, significantly below expected neutralization thresholds. This Structural Damage Severity resulted specifically from the resilient Defensive Position Fortification Level, whose hardened barriers dissipated blast pressure and fragment penetration, and critically from the Mortar Attack Authorized condition itself, which enabled the munitions to impact the target rather than being withheld. The administrative timeline influenced the authorization process but did not alter the physical Structural Damage Severity once the rounds impacted; only the fortification characteristics and the fact that the Mortar Attack Authorized status permitted ordnance release determined the percentage damaged.
