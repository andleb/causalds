# scene_000594

When Hurricane Marina slammed the coastal province, the torrential rains triggered a Secondary Road Landslide Occurrence, sending massive tons of earth and debris cascading across remote mountain routes and severing vital access corridors. Emergency infrastructure survey teams calculated that this geologic event directly increased the Proportion of Secondary Road Network Closed to 35%, blocking critical pathways that connected inland communities to the storm-ravaged coastline.

This elevated Proportion of Secondary Road Network Closed severely hampered ground-based emergency logistics, forcing the Air Ambulance Response Time to climb to 58 minutes as medical helicopters had to navigate longer flight paths to reach isolated villages that were normally accessible by ambulance. Simultaneously, the blocked terrestrial routes necessitated a complete pivot to sea-based supply chains, creating a Maritime Relief Supply Transit Delay of eight hours as cargo vessels rerouted to alternative ports and awaited suitable tidal conditions for unloading. Coastal safety officials simultaneously extended the Duration of Beach Closure to 48 hours, recognizing that the extensive Proportion of Secondary Road Network Closed prevented lifeguard crews and heavy debris-clearing equipment from reaching the shoreline promptly to assess sand stability and secure safe public access.
