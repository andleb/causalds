# scene_000224

In a multicenter randomized controlled trial for uncomplicated malaria, participants were first allocated via Randomized Treatment Assignment, a computer-generated 0/1 allocation sequence that eliminated selection bias and determined whether individuals would enter the active treatment protocol. Consequently, those assigned to the intervention arm underwent Artemether/Lumefantrine Administration, receiving the standard six-dose regimen with meals over 72 hours, while participants assigned to the comparator group did not receive the active compound. Following drug administration, the safety monitoring protocol required systematic assessment of neurological side effects. The research team recorded that patients who underwent Artemether/Lumefantrine Administration experienced a Headache Adverse Event coded as present, typically characterized by moderate intensity frontal headaches occurring within the first 24 hours post-initial dose. Conversely, among participants who did not receive Artemether/Lumefantrine Administration, the Headache Adverse Event remained absent throughout the 28-day follow-up period, demonstrating that the occurrence of headaches was directly contingent upon exposure to the antimalarial therapy.
