# scene_000255

During a scheduled cholecystectomy, the surgical team's Clinical Negligence manifested as failure to identify aberrant bile duct anatomy and subsequent improper clamping of vascular structures. This Clinical Negligence directly precipitated Critical Injury-specifically massive intraoperative hemorrhage and ischemic liver damage-that catastrophically destabilized the patient's hemodynamics. The Critical Injury subsequently drove Patient Mortality, as the volume of blood loss and organ necrosis exceeded the capabilities of massive transfusion protocols and surgical salvage. Furthermore, this same Clinical Negligence exerted a direct and independent effect on Patient Mortality through the anesthesiologist's concurrent failure to recognize acute bleeding cues and adjust ventilatory support, resulting in fatal hypoxia that would have occurred regardless of the severity of the physical trauma.
