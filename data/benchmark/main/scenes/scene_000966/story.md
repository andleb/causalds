# scene_000966

At 2:45 PM, a catastrophic failure in the oxidation reactor vessel produced a devastating blast with an Explosion Overpressure of 45 kPa that radiated across the petrochemical facility. The resulting shock wave instantly subjected the surrounding production units to stresses well beyond their design limits, causing Major Structural Damage as reinforced concrete walls fractured and steel support beams buckled under the intense compressive load. Simultaneously, the force of the blast sheared pressurized feed lines and ruptured solvent storage tanks, atomizing volatile organic compounds into a toxic aerosol cloud that permeated the ventilation system. Twelve operators working in the nearby control annex suffered Worker Chemical Inhalation Injury when they breathed in the high concentrations of benzene and toluene vapors violently dispersed by the pressure wave. Investigators later determined that both the severe structural compromise and the acute chemical exposures were triggered directly by the peak overpressure, rather than representing a sequential chain where one caused the other.
