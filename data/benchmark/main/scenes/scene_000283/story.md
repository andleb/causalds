# scene_000283

Simultaneously, the toxin's pore-forming activity allows ion leakage that dissipates the transmembrane electrochemical gradient, producing **Toxin-Induced Membrane Depolarization** of approximately -40 mV.

The cellular energy crisis manifests as **Intracellular ATP Concentration** plummeting to 60 µM, reflecting the convergence of damaged metabolism and environmental constraints. While **Extracellular Glucose Concentration** of 15 mM and **Dissolved Oxygen Tension** of 7 kPa normally fuel ATP synthesis through aerobic respiration, the **LdrD Toxic Protein Concentration** directly impairs ATP synthase complexes and accelerates ATP hydrolysis. Furthermore, the progressive **Toxin-Induced Membrane Depolarization** collapses the proton motive force required for oxidative phosphorylation, ensuring that despite ample oxygen and glucose availability, the bacterium cannot regenerate its **Intracellular ATP Concentration**.
