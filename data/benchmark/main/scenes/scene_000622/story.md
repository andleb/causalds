# scene_000622

At the industrial facility, the Production Line Power Rating, measured at 450 kW, directly determines the Manufacturing Batch Size that can be processed each cycle; this 450 kW capacity enables the line to handle batches of 10,000 units while generating significant Process Heat Generation, which registers at 120 kW due to the motor loads and friction inherent in high-capacity equipment. As the Manufacturing Batch Size increases to accommodate this power capacity, two distinct quality risks emerge: the Machine Operating Temperature climbs to 85°C because larger batches require extended processing cycles and increased mechanical stress on the thermal management systems, and simultaneously, the probability of a Raw Material Contamination Event rises sharply since handling 10,000 units creates more opportunities for cross-contamination during extended production runs and material transfers. When the Machine Operating Temperature exceeds safe thresholds combined with a Raw Material Contamination Event occurring during oversized batch processing, the facility faces an inevitable Product Recall Event, as excessive heat degrades product integrity while contamination violates safety standards, forcing the company to pull both compromised and potentially affected units from distribution channels.
