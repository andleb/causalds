# scene_000650

The region experienced an Annual Precipitation Deficit measured at 150 mm below the historical average throughout the hydrological year, creating significant stress on the basin's water balance. Meanwhile, the underlying Watershed Groundwater Storage Capacity-a deep geological characteristic representing the total volume of water naturally storable in underground aquifers-remained fixed at approximately 2.3 cubic kilometers based on bedrock composition and soil permeability, operating as an unobserved background constraint on the system's resilience.

When substantial Annual Precipitation Deficits combined with the inherently limited Watershed Groundwater Storage Capacity, the basin reached critical stress thresholds, resulting in the Hydrological Drought Occurrence being recorded as a binary positive event (1). This drought status indicated that stream flows and aquifer levels had fallen below operational normals sufficient to sustain regional water demands.

The municipality ultimately faced a Severe Municipal Water Shortage (coded as 1), triggered both by the confirmed Hydrological Drought Occurrence that restricted available surface and subsurface supplies, and independently by the constrained Watershed Groundwater Storage Capacity, which restricted the total volume of reserves accessible to urban infrastructure regardless of drought status. The combination of active drought conditions and constrained geological storage created a compounding effect that overwhelmed the region's ability to maintain adequate water delivery to municipal consumers.
