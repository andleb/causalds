# scene_000814

The Allied Amphibious Invasion functioned as a binary catalyst; once commanders shifted this variable from 0 to 1 and committed to the seaborne assault, they immediately triggered calculations for the Naval Bombardment Intensity required to secure the landing zones. Military logisticians translated the invasion decision into concrete firepower requirements, specifying the exact metric tons of ordnance that battleships and cruisers would expend against coastal fortifications and urban centers.

As the landing craft approached, the Naval Bombardment Intensity manifested as a measurable quantity of explosive steel delivered onto the city, with each metric ton of ordnance fracturing concrete and collapsing structures. Concurrently, the ongoing Allied Amphibious Invasion generated its own destructive footprint through the mechanized advance of ground forces and armored vehicles penetrating the urban core. The cumulative Urban Infrastructure Destruction, quantified as the percent destroyed of the city's functional buildings and critical utilities, emerged as the direct combined result of both the weight of naval shells and the physical presence of the invading troops.
