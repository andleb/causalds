# scene_000253

As the Intensity of Revolutionary Activity surged across Russia in 1917-measured by historians on an index score reaching nearly 80 by October-the fabric of military discipline began to unravel. This escalating revolutionary fervor, manifested in strikes, pacifist agitation, and anti-war demonstrations, directly caused the Military Desertion Rate to skyrocket from negligible levels to nearly 15 percent of the total armed forces by autumn. Soldiers, particularly in the Petrograd garrison and at the front lines, abandoned their posts as the revolutionary message of "peace, land, and bread" undermined their willingness to maintain the old order.

Simultaneously, the same Intensity of Revolutionary Activity-driven by Bolshevik agitation and economic desperation-directly precipitated a State of Widespread Civil Disorder, flipping the binary indicator from 0 to 1 as the provisional government lost control. This disorder materialized through armed demonstrations, seizures of property, and the breakdown of municipal services in major cities. However, the cascading Military Desertion Rate independently intensified this chaos, as hundreds of thousands of armed deserters roamed the countryside and urban centers, forming armed bands that further disrupted public order. When the Military Desertion Rate climbed above 10 percent, the State of Widespread Civil Disorder became entrenched, creating a self-reinforcing cycle where the absence of military authority removed the final barrier against complete societal breakdown.
