During the autumn monitoring period, coastal engineers documented that whenever a Severe Storm Event approached the region, the intense meteorological forcing generated substantially larger ocean swells, directly increasing the Maximum Wave Height measured in meters at the offshore gauge stations. The Severe Storm Event actively compromised shoreline stability through multiple destructive mechanisms including storm surge, sustained wind stress, and prolonged wave exposure duration, all of which directly determined whether Severe Beach Erosion Occurred along the vulnerable beach segments. Additionally, the specific magnitude of Maximum Wave Height represented the critical hydraulic forcing component, as taller waves carried exponentially greater energy for sediment transport; when the Maximum Wave Height exceeded critical thresholds of approximately three meters, it directly caused Severe Beach Erosion Occurred by scouring significant volumes of sand from the beach face and undermining protective dune structures, operating through physical mechanisms distinct from the storm's other characteristics.
